01 / interpretation

Executive summary

deterministic offline summary

This is a synthetic demonstration of a tumour-microenvironment single-cell analysis and does not describe a real sample. After automated QC, 1887 of 2000 cells passed (5.65% removed for low complexity or elevated mitochondrial content), leaving a median of 56 genes per cell.

Unsupervised clustering (kmeans(k=8)) resolved the infiltrate into interpretable populations: CD8 T (effector) 18.8%; Myeloid 16.3%; CD4 T (helper) 15.1%; CD8 T (exhausted) 13.8%; Tumour 13.5%; NK 8.5%; B 7.3%; Treg 6.7%. Recovered clusters agree well with the underlying populations (adjusted Rand index 0.964). The CD8+ T-cell compartment makes up roughly 32.6% of recovered cells.

Within that compartment, 70% of CD8+ T cells score high for a canonical exhaustion programme (PD-1, LAG-3, TIM-3, TIGIT, TOX), with a mean exhaustion z-score of 0.631. A substantial exhausted fraction alongside a measurable regulatory (Treg) presence is the kind of immunosuppressive signature that can blunt T-cell-engager activity. Testable follow-ups would be to (i) confirm co-expression of inhibitory receptors at single-cell resolution and (ii) ask whether checkpoint co-targeting shifts the effector-to-exhausted CD8 ratio.
